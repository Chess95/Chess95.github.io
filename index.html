Francesca Gray

I am an avian ecologist with a passion for applied research and data science in an ecological setting. I am passionate about understanding how environmental stressors impact wild bird populations, with particular focus on addressing data gaps critical to mitigating anthropogenic challenges in a rapidly changing environment.
I completed a NERC-funded PhD researching the effects of persistent organic pollutants (POPs) in wild bird populations at the University of Aberdeen, in collaboration with the Swiss Ornithological Institute (Vogelwarte). My main focus during my PhD was to understand how individuals vary in exposure and consequences of POPs across geographical, temporal, migratory and life-history data, in the Alpine swift (Tachymarptis melba).
I currently have four peer-reviewed publications (two first-author publications with a fifth as an advanced manuscript). You can see my list of publications here.
Last updated: 15/06/2025

Key academic interests

Ornithology

Wild bird populations are facing more anthropogenic challenges than ever. My research is dedicated to evaluating existing environmental damage, and to address the information gaps essential to support future impact mitigation.

Applied Research

Ensuring research can be applied to real-world challenges is vital. In this, I have ensured the publication of my research throughout my career, with all stages of my academic career resulting in peer-reviewed publications.

Data Science

During my PhD, I realised that my favourite aspect of research is data; from data management and data wrangling, to statisical analysis and data visualisation. I am committed to continuous learning and development in this sector.

Application of Technology in Research

The application of developing technologies to biological sciences opens up research questions we could not previously answer. I have worked with a behavioural recording system, RFID tracking and GLS loggers to answer a range of questions.
